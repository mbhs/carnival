The Piece

The Carnival of the Animals is a humorous musical suite of fourteen movements by the French Romantic composer Camille Saint-Saëns.
The Carnival of the Animals is supposed to be fun. Each of the suite's 14 movements introduces us to a different animal or group of animals, with a small number of instruments mimicking their voices or the way they move. Starting with the lion's roar and slowing to reflect the elephant's bulk, Saint-Saens pokes fun at the music of his time.
The piece has become one of Saint-Saëns's best-known works.

The Outstanding Blair Chamber Orchestra will play the piece, accompanied by a simultaneously running video for young children linking the instruments and animals.

Instrument Petting Zoo

Member of Blair Bands and Orchestras will be on hand with their instruments so kids can see them, learn about them, and can try them out.
We expect to have a full compliment of band and orchestra instruments, as well as pianos. This is something the National Symphony Orchestra does, but it costs way more and is all the way downtown!

The festival will feature face-painting, allowing your child to express their creativity in a fun and delightful manner! All materials are 100% safe and we will have trained volunteers on-hand for assistance.

Cupcake Walk

Don't miss the chance to win a cupcake! The game begins when the music starts; children walk around in a circle as the music is playing. When the music stops, the children stop. A volunteer will call out a carnival image and the child standing on the corresponding image wins a cupcake!
